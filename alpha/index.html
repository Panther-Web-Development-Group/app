What is PantherWeb?

The Panther Web Development Group is a registered student organization that allows all Georgia State University students with a vested interest in web development, web development, and coding to learn how to create websites and applications, apply their web development skills, and decide whether to publish the finished product.

Mission Statement

PantherWeb strives to spread awareness of web development at Georgia State University and create a space for students to learn about web development and apply those skills by building (or improving on) unique websites.

Students can collaborate with their peers to discuss their passions and ideas, which assists students with developing better websites and applications. We find that websites created by students in PantherWeb can serve as wonderful additions to their portfolios.

What does the Panther Web Development Group do?

  • Educate Georgia State University students about creating a website, the components of a website, and using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
  • Create collaborative small-scale and large-scale web development projects and encourage our members to join.
  • Develop web development skills required to solve real-world problems in the field.
  • Host code demonstrations in most general body meetings and events.
  • Support students in building their networks and expand their portfolios.
